Diversity

Initial reactions in a small group of strangers may include a mix of curiosity about everyone’s background, culture, personality, and more to be mentioned.

Each group member might find themselves judging or evaluating their own social standing in their assigned group, such as trying to make a good first impression towards the group.

A sense of anticipation to work on an objective as a group gives a spark of motivation to show the group that you are engaged.

Complex Problems

In the IT field, complex problems could arise, and teamwork would be an ideal strategy. A lot could go wrong; therefore, the collective intelligence and resource pooling of a group would grant better assessed solutions from various viewpoints and technical background, within whether they will hit a roadblock in the future because of this solution and amongst other things such as risk management, pros and cons. Dealing with it individually could be a missed opportunity on skill and knowledge development. It could also affect the quality of the solution in a negative way, where the diverse perspective and technical expertise would positively increase the quality of the solution.

Favors working in a group

Small groups often include individuals with different backgrounds, experiences and areas of skill. This diversity of perspectives can lead to further discussions and innovative solutions. This favor can encourage group members to challenge each other’s assumptions, leading to deeper insights, making it easier to approach the problem as a group.

Sharing of individual skills, knowledge and expertise allows for promotion of collaborative learning and skill sharing in a small group setting.

When individuals work towards the same goal, especially as a small group. It gives a higher level of motivation in terms of engagement and dedication to perform well to the group’s objective.

Critical Thinking

Disagreement is beneficial because it brings a variety of viewpoints/perspectives to the table. It is beneficial when taking on difficult challenges that require a lot of teamwork. When people with different viewpoints challenge each other's ideas and thoughts it sparks innovation and critical thinking among the group. This process often reveals flaws in assumptions and opens a deeper exploration of the problem. Healthy disagreement is an essential part of good-problem solving because it allows the team to develop a larger variety of options to account for, while developing stronger solutions.

Handling group conflict

The optimal approach to addressing group conflict involves handling it on an individual basis with the person who has missed the meeting(s). The objective is to comprehend the reasons behind their absence. Efforts would be made to resolve the situation by reaching out to the absent individual, considering that the absence could be due to medical reasons or personal circumstances. To tackle this concern, options like rescheduling the meeting for another day or conducting it online would be explored. In cases where the meeting holds significant importance and the absent co-worker cannot attend, measures would be taken to ensure that the necessary information is provided to them, along with a summary of the meeting.
